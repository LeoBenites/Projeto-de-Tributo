Nicholas Wilton
Helped hundreds of children escape from concentration camps

The Great Hero of World War II

Sir Nicholas Winton was a Briton who organized the rescue of 669 mostly Jewish children in former Czechoslovakia before they were deported to Nazi concentration camps, saving them from certain death in 1939 before the outbreak of World War II I prepared immigration papers, found English families willing to adopt the children, and still got money to transport them to London. In the process, he did not hesitate to bribe German agents to turn a blind eye to what was happening. Between March and August 1939, in the face of concentration camp threats, Winton worked to get 669 children to leave Czechoslovakia to live in England..
